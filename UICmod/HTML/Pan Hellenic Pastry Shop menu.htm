Menu for Pan Hellenic Pastry Shop

Pan Pastries

  • Baklava (pc.) $2.45

    It's made of alternating layers of lightly buttered phyllo dough 40 leaves in all and ground walnuts. After it's baked we drench it in our signature honey syrup

  • Galaktoboureko (pc.) $2.45

    A greek dessert staple. Our milk custard with the slightest hint of lemon, encased in butter phyllo dough. It's baked to golden brown and covered in honey syrup

  • Kataifi (pc.) $2.45

    Rolls of shredded wheat glazed over with our clarified butter that are filled with ground walnuts. They are then baked to golden brown and soaked in honey syrup

  • Saragli (pc.) $2.45

    A pastry with similar ingredients to our classic baklava that is cut into a pinwheel shape. We fill our saragli with choice of pecans or pistachios

  • Floyera (pc.) $1.85

    A baklava alternative, filled with a mix of ground walnuts and almonds. Floyera means flute in greek and these pastries are shaped like flutes. They are easier to handle than baklava and so are a very popular for party platters. We also offer floyeres that are dipped in dark chocolate instead of honey

  • Floyera (Chocolate pc.) $1.95

    A baklava alternative, filled with a mix of ground walnuts and almonds. Floyera means flute in greek and these pastries are shaped like flutes. They are easier to handle than baklava and so are a very popular for party platters. We also offer floyeres that are dipped in dark chocolate instead of honey

  • Karidopita (pc.) $2.45

    A walnut honey cake with hints of cinnamon, clove and lemon

  • Diples (Large) $2.45

    An airy egg based pastry dough that is hand rolled while being fried. It's then soaked in honey and sprinkled with cinnamon and ground walnuts

  • Diples (Small) $1.60

    An airy egg based pastry dough that is hand rolled while being fried. It's then soaked in honey and sprinkled with cinnamon and ground walnuts

  • Samous (pc.) $2.75

    It's a roll of thicker than usual phyllo dough filled with toasted almonds and sesame. It's baked, sometimes with olive oil and sometimes with clarified butter

Cokkies - Biscuits

  • Koulouria (dz.) $5.95

  • Kourabiethes (lb.) $10.75

    A greek staple on the dessert table. They are almond butter cookies with bits of almond in the dough. We also add a hint of clove. We then cover them in mounds of powdered sugar

  • Melomakarona (lb.) $10.75

    Another greek classic. Honey and spice walnut cookies. We use clove, cinnamon and just a little lemon zest to give them their singular taste

  • Amigdalota (lb.) $14.25

    Almond paste cookies that we offer in various shapes. We also offer them with an apricot jam filling

  • Louie's Cookies (lb.) $14.25

  • Paximadia (lb.) $9.95

  • Macaroons (pc.) $1.15

  • Shortbread Petit Fours (lb.) $9.95

  • Moustokouloura (dz.) $6.50

    These non-dairy biscuits are made from flour, olive oil and grape must

Specialty

  • Bougatsa (pc.) $2.75

    Our milk custard wrapped in phyllo dough

  • Milopita (pc.) $3.25

    Our version of apple strudel made with phyllo dough of course instead of a puff pastry dough. Inside you will find slices of fresh apples along with some golden raisins and a mixture of nuts. It's also covered in powdered sugar and cinnamon

  • Pasteli (pc.) $1.75

    Pasteli is a simple sweet snack made from sesame seeds and honey

  • Karioka (pc.) $2.25

    A chocolate concoction that can best be described as brownie-like in texture but we add walnuts, almonds and strawberry jam to the chocolate dough. We then add a dark chocolate coat to each

  • Loukoumades (Order) $5.75

    Loukoumades are light and airy fried dough dumplings. After frying them we immerse them in warm honey and sprinkle with cinnamon and crushed walnuts

  • Tsoureki (lb.) $7.45

    Tsoureki is more cake than bread. It's a specialty bread that we make during holidays the year round. It's egg based and is spiced with mastic resin from chios, mahlab from syria, and cinnamon. We sweeten it with fresh orange juice for a truly original taste
